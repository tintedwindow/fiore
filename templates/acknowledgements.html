Thank you for making this possible
This is a CS50 project.
Perhaps, the course that shaped me as a programmer and more so, a problem solver ...
When I started thinking of a project idea, initially I wanted to create a mini version of Geoguessr because I loved it so much (basically a game where you are placed in a random street view around the world and you have to guess where you are), and when I saw it was possible via OpenGuessr, I was eager to start my final project for CS50. However, I soon realized that Geoguessr relies on Google Street View, and using it's API requires a Google Cloud account, which involves billing and a credit card- something I didn't have at that time. This was a frustrating setback, especially since the idea to create a mini Geoguessr had excited me so much.
It was also around the time Apple was having one of their WWDC events, and me having watched that, YouTube was recommending me older WWDC videos. And that's when I saw WWDC 2013.
'The first thing we ask is what do we want people to feel?'
That line deeply resonated with me. As someone who always saw exploring street view as a means of escape, a calming feeling waiting for me in a random street of the world especially Italy, I knew what I wanted my project to make people feel.
I also had started journaling, thanks to Jade, Nathaniel, Sophie, Ali and Sadia. At first, the experience was mixed since I had just started, but soon everything began to fall into place. What made it easier to journal was on Ali's suggestion to try an app called Day One, a digital journal, where on a free account, you were limited to one entry a day. And this free plan limitation surprisingly ended up helping me in simplifying my journaling habits!
This limitation and the new found simplicity ultimately led me to a vague idea I sketched on my Keeps one night that if I could combine the feeling of looking at a place or a memory and associating it with a day, it could work. Then thus, the idea for Fiore was born: Framing Instances On Rectangles Everyday! This project made me realize that there's so much to learn for me and it also made me realize that I infact did when I finally solved each challange whether it was flying the nett and setting up local development and making Flask work, or going from a table tag to divisions that represent days in Fiore now :)
I have always been a Notion user and I've always admired it for it's simplicity and organization. I remember, the first time visiting their website and being so inspired by it that I used to think that one day I would make one like this. This played a key role in building the design for this website, reflecting the calmness of a simple and organized journal app with added hints of Italy, Fiore.
